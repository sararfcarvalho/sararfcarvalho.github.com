Sprint Reader is an online magazine covering culture, art, music, cinema, technology, etc. And more recently running, it has in mind a millenial generation that lives permanently in a sprint. This brand also sells Activewear and Running products on its website.

Joana

30 yo, Editor, Braga

Joana wants to be allways informed about what is happening in the world but has not many time to do that.

She thinks that the information is very dispersed, many times doesn't find what has more insterest to her.

Many times has no internet connection.

Frustrations VS Solutions


Some frustrations from our personas lead us to different solutions.

  • - What if I don't have connection to the Internet? Give the user the possibility to save articles.

  • - I feel like I don't have time to be informed of everything. The user can see articles stories and then decide if he wants to read it.

  • - I don't like reading too long articles. Podcasts.

  • - I like to give feedback. The user can give feedback in the detail product and in the articles page.

  • - I feel like everything is very standard these days. Product customization.

Wireframes & User Testing


After studying the user flow and some sketchs we started with the wireframes, we made user tests and we realized that the user needed more control on the cart, like edit colors or sizes.

The test also showed us that the user couldn't differentiate the common product of the custom one. And in the checkout phase he wanted to login or register besides insert all the info to purchase the products.
